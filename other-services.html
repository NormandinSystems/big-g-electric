Is Your Home Truly Protected?

For the Big G Electric team, the safety of our customers is our top priority. That’s why we offer professional installation and maintenance services for smoke and carbon monoxide detectors in Montreal and Laval.

You might think most homes are already equipped with a smoke detector, right? Many are, but not all are fully functional!

We strongly advise you to check yours and make sure it is perfectly operational. Indeed, many household chemicals do not emit smoke when they burn, and an old detector may not be able to detect these dangerous fumes.

Carbon Monoxide Detectors

Carbon monoxide (also known as CO) is an invisible, tasteless, scentless gas that you could be exposed to at any time in your own home without knowing.

The most common source of CO gas is an improperly installed or damaged gas appliance, such as a gas-powered range or oven, a damaged water heater, or improper ventilation in your garage—especially in the colder winter months when warming up cars.

Big G Electric Recommends:

Installation of a carbon monoxide detector in each bedroom of your home. We also recommend installing alarms that provide both an audible and visual alert to ensure rapid, safe evacuation.

Smoke Detectors

Having a reliable smoke detector system is an extremely important addition to your family’s safety. Big G Electric offers installation and maintenance on cutting-edge smoke detection and alarm systems.

In decades past, smoke detectors were individual units. This meant a fire could easily cut off escape routes before the alarm nearest you went off.

Interconnected Alarms

With interconnected alarms, when one smoke detector is triggered, all the alarms in the network signal simultaneously, giving you the precious seconds you need to escape calmly and safely.

Maintenance Tips

Keep your home's defenses active.

5-Year Lifespan

It is important to note that most smoke and carbon monoxide detectors have an average lifespan of just five (5) years.

Yearly & Monthly Checks

Detector batteries should be replaced yearly. We also highly recommend testing all your alarm and detection systems at least monthly.
